Ensign Fleet Championship — William Meredith Wood Memorial Trophy

Ensign Fleet 63 — BBYRA Season Champion

William Meredith Wood Memorial Trophy

Awarded to the BBYRA Ensign Fleet 63 Season Champion — First awarded 2012

William M. Wood and his crew and wife Peggy Keyser started winning trophies on Barnegat Bay in the 1910s. Bill won BBYRA Championships in his 18ft. Catboat Suds in 1927 and his Barnegat 17, Grandmother Honey, in 1967. In the 1950s he helped develop the current BBYRA Barrel Courses. In 1972 he bought Pearson Ensign #176, Demitasse, and in 1973 formed Ensign Fleet 63. Over the next twelve years he helped the fleet grow to 23 racing boats. Bill taught 6 grandchildren to sail, race, crab, and overnight cruise on the Demitasse.

Flying Scot Fleet Championship — Ralph and Ethel Manee Perpetual Trophy

Flying Scot — BBYRA Season Champion

Ralph and Ethel Manee Perpetual Trophy

Flying Scot — BBYRA Championship

Awarded annually to the BBYRA Flying Scot fleet season champion. Ralph Manee of Shore Acres Yacht Club was himself a dominant force in the fleet, winning the championship eight consecutive times from 1983 through 1989 and again in 1972. Peter Sayia of SPYC has won the trophy a remarkable 16 times.

The fleet was not scheduled 1973–1982.

East Coast Midgets — Single Handed Championship

Optimist Pram — Single Handed Midget Champion

Single Handed Midget Champion — Optimist Pram

Barnegat Bay Yacht Racing Association

This was originally a single championship that has been split into two separate competitions — Single Handed and Double Handed — each with history back to the beginning. The single handed championship is sailed in the Optimist Pram. Robin Brown of BYC won the championship five times in the 1950s and 1960s. Peter Commette of MYC also won three times.

Records from 1982–1989 are incomplete.

East Coast Midgets — Double Handed Championship

Club 420 — Double Handed Midget Champion

Double Handed Midget Champion — Club 420

Barnegat Bay Yacht Racing Association

The double handed championship is sailed in the Club 420. Records extend back to 1951, making this one of the longest-running junior championship records in the BBYRA. Notable winners include Robin Brown of BYC, Jay Lutz of MCYC, and Peggy Roesler of TRYC. The championship was not awarded in 2020–2022 due to Covid-19.
