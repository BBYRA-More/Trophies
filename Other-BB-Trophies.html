Women’s Championship Trophy — Adams Trophy

BBYRA Elimination — US Sailing Women’s Championship

Awarded to the winner of the BBYRA elimination leading to the US Sailing Women’s Championship (Mrs. Charles Francis Adams Trophy). Awarded when two or more competitors enter; if only one competitor enters the BBYRA elimination and represents BBYRA in the Area C Semifinal, the trophy and flag are still awarded.

Jan C. O’Malley of MYC won the national championship in 1969 and 1970. Debbie Freeman’s crew of BYC won the nationals in 1974, and Betsy Gelenitis’s MCYC crew won in 1984. Cynthia B. Wesson was the first Area C champion in 1975 and went on to win the national title in 1977. The elimination was not held from 1993 through 2004 and again from 2006 through 2015.

Yachtswoman of the Year

TRYC

Virginia Read Memorial Trophy

Yachtswoman of the Year — Toms River Yacht Club

The Yachtswoman of the Year shall be selected for her outstanding service, achievements, participation in, contribution to, and promotion of the sport of sailing in the Barnegat Bay area. Said qualifications may have been over a protracted period of time. Eligibility is limited to female participants of the Ocean County College sailing program and members of BBYRA member yacht clubs. Qualified candidates may be eligible posthumously. Nominations are submitted in writing to the Fleet Captain of Toms River Yacht Club no later than Labor Day. The perpetual trophy is a bowl hand painted by Stephen P. Weston in 2005.

The MYTH Trophy

Barnegat Bay Charities Inc. — Dress Ship Competition

Barnegat Bay Charities Inc. presents a “dress ship” competition open to all types of sailing vessels owned by members of the BBYRA. The judges will select the competing sailing vessel, at anchor and at “dress ship,” that best displays the spirit of the occasion and whose vessel is presented in a clean, orderly, and seamanlike manner. The MYTH Trophy was graciously donated in 2003 by members of the MYTH 1889 Syndicate from BHYC. The 100-year-old oversized sterling silver flask is beautifully hand-engraved with “MYTH Trophy” and the MYTH 1889 pennant, mounted on a double pedestal base with engraving plaques. The perpetual trophy resides at the winner’s BBYRA yacht club for one year, or until the next Barnegat Bay Ball.
